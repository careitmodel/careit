## Purpose

The Lifecycle Overview ensures sustainable operational capability of digital systems across their entire period of use.

It prevents infrastructure that is technically stable but strategically obsolete.

The artifact supports the healthcare provider’s regulatory operator responsibility by ensuring that lifecycle control remains visible and actively governed.


## Guiding Question

> Is this system sustainably operable, maintainable, and regulatorily compliant across its entire lifecycle?


## Structural Effect

The Lifecycle Overview:

- creates transparency regarding version status and support cycles  
- makes update and replacement strategies explicit  
- prevents uncontrolled legacy structures  
- highlights manufacturer dependencies  
- strengthens long-term regulatory and operational stability  

It operationalizes P6 – Sustainable Operational Capability.

Without structured lifecycle governance, stability becomes reactive rather than strategic.


## When Is It Applied?

- During strategic infrastructure planning  
- During budget planning cycles  
- In release and upgrade strategies  
- When evaluating manufacturer dependencies  
- During end-of-life or replacement decisions  
- In constellation-wide lifecycle assessments  

The Lifecycle Overview is not an asset register.  
It is a governance-level lifecycle control instrument.


## Typical Misapplications

- Focusing exclusively on technical maintenance  
- Lacking structured replacement planning  
- Failing to connect lifecycle decisions with system architecture  
- Ignoring regulatory developments  
- Treating lifecycle topics as purely operational matters  

Lifecycle governance is a leadership responsibility,  
not merely a technical task.
