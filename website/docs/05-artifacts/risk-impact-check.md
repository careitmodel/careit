# Clinical Risk Impact Check

## Purpose

The Clinical Risk Impact Check structures the deliberate evaluation of patient-relevant risks associated with digital systems.

It prevents implicit risk acceptance and creates transparency in safety-critical decision processes.

The artifact ensures that regulatory operator responsibility (healthcare provider accountability) is consciously exercised when risk-relevant decisions are made.

---

## Guiding Question

> What patient-relevant risks arise from this system — and how are they consciously assumed and governed?

---

## Structural Effect

The Clinical Risk Impact Check:

- identifies systemic risks within the clinical system constellation  
- distinguishes technical disturbances from clinical impact  
- makes risk decisions explicit and traceable  
- anchors responsibility for risk acceptance at leadership level  
- strengthens regulatory operator accountability  

It operationalizes P5 – Patient Safety as Normative Boundary.

Without this artifact, risk is often managed operationally but not consciously governed.

---

## When Is It Applied?

- Prior to commissioning new systems  
- Before major system modifications  
- During release planning  
- During architectural changes  
- When systemic constellation risks are identified  
- During structured re-evaluation of existing systems  

The Clinical Risk Impact Check is not a technical checklist.  
It is a governance-level risk decision instrument.

---

## Typical Misapplications

- Reducing it to an IT security assessment  
- Equating it with regulatory conformity  
- Using it only reactively after incidents  
- Failing to document the actual risk decision  
- Delegating risk evaluation exclusively to technical teams  

Regulatory compliance is necessary but not sufficient for patient safety.  
Patient safety requires explicit organizational risk decisions.
