## Scope of Version 1.2.0

Version 1.2.0 includes:

- The eight CARE-IT Foundational Principles (P1–P8)  
- The six architectural domains (D1–D6)  
- The dual-perspective maturity model (principles + domains)  
- The indicator-based maturity logic without numerical aggregation  
- The consolidated artifact architecture  
- The structural separation of Adoption Governance and Framework Governance  
- The formalized Framework Governance model  
- The refined Controlled Open contribution logic  
- The updated versioning and translation governance model  
- The end-to-end application example  
- The complete glossary and reference structure  

This version represents the currently valid reference edition of the CARE-IT framework.

## Character of Version 1.2.0

Version 1.2.0 is:

- Architecturally consolidated  
- Governance-complete  
- Terminologically harmonized  
- Structurally stabilized  
- Internationalized (English master version)  
- Published under a Controlled Open philosophy  

It establishes CARE-IT as a governed reference architecture  
for digital clinical infrastructure.

---

## Changes Compared to Version 1.1.1

Version 1.2.0 is a **minor release under semantic versioning** and includes:

- Introduction of a formal Framework Governance model  
- Clear separation between Framework Governance and Adoption Governance  
- Formal definition of English as the authoritative source language  
- Structured translation governance logic  
- Refined contribution philosophy (Controlled Open model)  
- Harmonization of terminology across principles, domains, and artifacts  
- Architectural clarification of innovation from an operator perspective  
- Structural reorganization of navigation and governance layers  

No foundational principles were modified.

The normative architecture remains stable.

---

## Versioning Logic

CARE-IT follows semantic versioning:

- **Major** – Structural redefinition or conceptual paradigm shift  
- **Minor** – Architectural extension, governance consolidation, or structural refinement  
- **Patch** – Editorial, formatting, or clarification updates without normative impact  

Version governance is defined in the Framework Governance section.
