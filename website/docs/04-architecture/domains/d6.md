---
title: D6 – Innovation Capability & Evolution
---
## Purpose of the Domain

D6 ensures that new digital solutions can be integrated into the existing clinical care environment in a repeatable, timely, safe, and sustainable manner.

Innovation must neither occur without control nor be structurally blocked.  
Digital evolution must be integrable into the existing architecture, responsibility structures, and operational reality.

This domain operationalizes Principle P8 – Innovation Capability from the Operator Perspective – at the organizational level.


## Core Governance Question

> Is the organization structurally capable of integrating new solutions into clinical operations in a controlled manner?

D6 does not address innovation projects as isolated events.  
It addresses the structural capability for continuous evolution.


## Problem Context

Organizations often face two extremes:

**Uncontrolled innovation:**

- Project-driven introduction  
- Missing integration into the clinical system constellation  
- Unclear responsibilities  
- Operational instability after go-live  

**Innovation blockage:**

- Excessively long decision cycles  
- Lack of structured evaluation logic  
- Overemphasis on stability  
- Fear of regulatory risk  

Both conditions create structural weakness:

- either fragile infrastructure  
- or technological stagnation  

Innovation without architecture creates instability.  
Stability without innovation capability leads to loss of relevance.


## Structural Requirements

A mature expression of D6 requires:

- A clear evaluation logic for new solutions  
- Binding assessment of clinical effectiveness and risk  
- Integration of new systems into existing clinical system constellations  
- Transparent allocation of responsibility during introduction  
- Structured project-to-operation transitions  
- Capability for controlled scaling  

Innovation must remain structurally aligned with D1–D5.


## Relationship to Other Domains

- D1 evaluates clinical effectiveness of new solutions.  
- D2 integrates them into the existing constellation.  
- D3 defines responsibility structures.  
- D4 assesses patient-relevant risks.  
- D5 ensures long-term operational sustainability.  

D6 connects stability with evolution.


## Typical Misconceptions

- “Innovation means rapid implementation.”  
- “Stability and innovation are mutually exclusive.”  
- “New solutions can be integrated later.”  
- “Pilot projects do not require structural embedding.”  

Innovation is not an exception to the system —  
it is part of the system.


## Indicators of Structural Stability

D6 is structurally stable when:

- Innovation follows a defined evaluation logic  
- New solutions are systemically integrated  
- Introduction and operation are not decoupled  
- Evolution is reproducible and governable  

D6 is weakly developed when:

- Innovation is opportunistic  
- Projects remain isolated  
- Operational issues emerge only after introduction  
- Technological developments cannot be structurally absorbed  


D6 ensures that digital clinical infrastructure not only remains stable,  
but evolves in a controlled and sustainable manner.
